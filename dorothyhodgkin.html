Dorothy Hodgkin

Nobel Prize Winner 1964

Dorothy was born in 1910 to English parents in Cairo.

She attended a grammar school in Suffolk where she was one of two girls allowed to study chemistry.

She studied chemistry at Somerville College, Oxford, graduating in 1932 and moving to Cambridge for her doctorate at Newnham College.

She later returned to Oxford and began her independent academic career researching x-ray crystallography.

She was awarded the Nobel prize in 1964 for her discovery of the structure of vitamin B12 and remains to this day, the only British female scientist to be awarded a Nobel prize.

Hodgkin was fiercley political and a lifelong Labour supporter, despite this, her former student Margaret Thatcher kept a portrait of her in her office in Downing Street.

She died in 1994 of a stroke. In her memory, the Royal Society awards fellowships in her name to those who require a flexible research career due to personal circumstances.
