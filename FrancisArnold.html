Francis Arnold

Nobel Prize Winner 2018

Follow @francisarnold

Francis Arnold was born in 1956 in Pennsylvania.

She studied a bachelors in mechanical and aerospace engineering at Princeton University. She then moved to the University of California Berkley where she gained her doctorate in chemical engineering in 1985.

She has remained at Berkley for the rest of her research career.

She was awared the Nobel prize in 2018 for directed evolution, a process used to create enzymes with improved or new functions.

Francis is a big supporter of women in STEM initiatives, even appearing as herself in The Big Bang Theory to bring attention to female scientists.
