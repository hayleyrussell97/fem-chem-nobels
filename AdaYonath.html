Ada Younath

Nobel Prize Winner 2009

Follow @adayonath

Ada Yonath was born in Jerusalem to Polish parents. The family later moved to Tel Aviv, where she went to a prestigous high school on scholarship.

She studied a bachelors degree in chemistry at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem and later gained her doctorate from the Weizmann Insitute for Science.

Ada completed postdoctoral research placements at Carnegie Mellon University and MIT.

Returning to Israel, she established the only protein crystallography lab in Israel. She focused her career on the mechanisms underlying protein biosynthesis.

She was awarded the nobel prize in 2009 for her work on the structure and function of the ribosome.

She is the first Israeli woman to win a bovel prize, the first woman from the Middle East to win a scientific Nobel prize and the first woman in 45 to have been awarded the chemistry Nobel Prize.
